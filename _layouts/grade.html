Course Policies

Late Work

All assignments are due by 11:55 PM on the stated due date and must be submitted via Canvas. Assignments will be accepted up to three days past the deadline with a penalty of 20% per calendar day late:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

Barriers and Accommodations

This course is committed to accessibility and inclusion. If you face or anticipate any barriers to full participation and fair evaluation, please communicate your needs with the instructor early, preferably during the first week. You may also contact the Accessible Education Center (AEC) at 360 Oregon Hall, 541-346-1155, or via email at uoaec@uoregon.edu for assistance:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Academic Honesty

All submitted work must be your own unless otherwise specified. Collaboration must be acknowledged. Plagiarism, including copying from peers or external sources without proper citation, will be reported. Academic misconduct is defined by the University Student Conduct Code (conduct.uoregon.edu). Protect your work from being copied by managing file permissions on shared systems:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

Academic Disruption Due to Campus Emergency

In the event of a campus emergency that disrupts normal academic activities, adjustments to course requirements, deadlines, or grading may occur. Updates will be communicated via Canvas and email. Students should monitor announcements and continue reading and working on assignments as outlined in the syllabus:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Prohibited Discrimination and Harassment Reporting

The instructor is a designated and mandatory reporter. If you experience or witness prohibited discrimination or harassment—including gender-based violence—resources are available at safe.uoregon.edu, respect.uoregon.edu, and investigations.uoregon.edu. For direct support, contact the Office of Investigations and Civil Rights Compliance at 541-346-3123, or the 24/7 hotline at 541-346-SAFE:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.
